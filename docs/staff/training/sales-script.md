# Sales Script

Phone conversation guide for handling wedding and event inquiries at Silver Sycamore. Follow this script to gather necessary information and set up tour appointments.

## Opening

1. Answer the phone with a warm greeting
2. Say: "Good morning/afternoon, thank you for calling Silver Sycamore. How may I assist you?"

## Pricing Inquiry Flow

When a caller asks about pricing, follow these steps to gather the information needed for an accurate quote.

### Step 1: Request Permission to Ask Questions

1. Say: "So I may assist you best, may I ask a few questions?"
2. Wait for confirmation

### Step 2: Introduction

1. Say: "This is [your name], may I ask whom I am speaking with?"
2. Note the caller's name

### Step 3: Event Type

1. Ask: "Ms./Mr. [Name], what type of event will you be having? Wedding and reception?"
2. Note the event type

### Step 4: Date Availability

1. Say: "So we don't get too far along, let me check the date you are looking to hold your event."
2. Note the requested date

### Step 5: Guest Count

1. Ask: "And approximately how many guests will you be inviting?"
2. Note the guest count

### Step 6: Ceremony Location

1. Ask: "Are you wanting your ceremony in our beautiful Chapel or at one of our outdoor Garden Gazebos?"
2. Note the preference

### Step 7: Menu Selection

1. Ask: "How gourmet of a menu do you want to serve your guests - light appetizer? Full dinner?"
2. Note the meal preference

### Step 8: Marketing Source

1. Ask: "And finally, how did you hear about Silver Sycamore? And what caught your attention about us?"
2. Note the referral source

## Provide Pricing Estimate

After gathering information, provide a price range.

1. Say: "Very good, thank you for all this information. It helps me to give you an idea of a range of pricing for you."

2. Explain: "We are an all-inclusive facility including catering, cake, DJ, linens and such. Our pricing for:"
   - 20 guests starts around **$2,500**
   - 50 guests starts around **$5,000**
   - 100 guests starts around **$10,000** (approximately **$100 per person**) and goes up depending on guest count and menu selection

3. Ask: "Is this somewhere within what you had realistically thought you would be?"

### If Budget Does Not Match

1. Thank them for their time
2. End the call politely

### If Budget Matches

1. Ask: "Great! Now have you been out to see our venue or Sycamoreville yet?"

## Schedule Tour Appointment

If they have not visited and budget aligns, schedule a tour.

1. Say: "Let's set up an appointment to come tour our grounds and go over packages in greater detail. Does that sound good?"

2. Explain: "We like to make it when both the bride and groom and anyone else making this major decision can attend."

3. Ask: "When is best for all to attend - weekdays or weekends?"

4. Offer options: "We have appointments available at [time] and [time]. Which works best for you?"

5. Collect: Date, time, contact phone number, and email

6. Confirm: "Alright, we have you set for [date] at [time]. You will be meeting with Jackie."

## Pre-Appointment Instructions

1. Say: "We will send a text and email reminder the day before your tour. If you cannot make it, please let us know as soon as possible because we stay booked for appointments."

2. Encourage preparation: "In the meantime, be thinking about details of what your perfect wedding looks like because I know Jackie will be asking you this during your appointment."

## Provide Directions

1. Explain: "Let me explain where the office is on the grounds."

2. Give directions: "When you turn onto the grounds, you will drive towards the town 'Sycamoreville' and park there. Come to the 'Post Office' building - the Tour office is inside."

## Closing

1. Say: "We look forward to seeing you. Thank you and have a nice day."
